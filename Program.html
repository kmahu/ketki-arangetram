Performances

MALLARI


Mallari is an invocatory piece whose origin comes from temples wherein a diety was carried in a procession. The rhythmic beats guide the dancer’s steps using only sound and no words. Commonly performed in the beginning of the performance, the Mallari includes a greeting to the Gods, Guru, and audience.

PRANAVAKAARAM


Another name of Lord Ganesha, Pranavakaaram is the form of OM and the following phrase “Siddhi Vinayakam” celebrates him as the giver of blessings. Composer OotukkaaDu VenkaTasubbaiyyar wrote of the Lord’s smiling face and glorious dance to the beat of drums. Known to grant boons and remove obstacles, Ganesha is honored in this dance through a mixture of Bhava and Nritta.

JATISWARAM


Composed by the Tanjore Quartet, Jatiswaram is an extremely traditional piece in the Bharatanatyam margam. The dance is beautiful in its simplicity and frames the ragam and taalam to which patterns are performed.

RAMAYANAM VARNAM


The centerpiece of the Arangetram is a full depiction of the story of Rama and his victory against the evil Ravana. This varnam follows Rama from his first encounter with aruras to his adventures with hanuman, all while weaving fast-paced jathis into the intricate storytelling. A test of stamina and skill, the varnam is a captivating journey on stage that embodies the soul of dance.

SHANKARA SRI GIRI


Shankara Sri Giri is a powerful dance that highlights Lord Shiva’s cosmic dance and the flowing waters of Ganga in his matted hair. He is described performing in front of the ganas alongside Goddess Parvati to the beats of Nandi.

OMKAR SWARUPA


In this tribute to the Guru, who’s worth can never be counted or reached, the dancer conveys the wealth of knowledge and enlightenment that is within a teacher. It isn’t possible to put devotion to one’s guru into words and is instead shown through traditional tales. One detailing the exchange between Arjuna and Krishna, in front of the battle field, and the other retelling the tale of Sant Eknath, the composer, and his disciple “Polya”.

THILLANA


Thillana is a mostly nritta piece, composed in the ragam Kaapi with brisk adavu and footwork in a pallavi-anupallavi-charanam structure. Typically done at the end of a program, the dance is exciting to watch and perform, ending with a charanam that praises the Devi who resides everywhere we can see and in nature.

AIGIRI NANDINI


Aigiri Nandini celebrates the Goddess Parvati as a Mangalam, which is the final dance. The opening lines of Aigiri Nandani are translated to "O Daughter of the Mountain" a popular stotrum honoring the devi, slayer of the arusa Mahishasura. In the piece, Mahishasura runs rampant on earth and hearing the pleas of her devotees, Goddess Parvati descends from the mountain to stop him. The beautiful devi baits the asura into fighting her and reveals her true might in the height of battle.
